# Personal site brief — Danny Bolella

A single-page personal site for **Danny Bolella, Lead iOS Engineer**. To be built in Claude Design using the existing Captain SwiftUI design system as the visual foundation, with the brand identity intentionally pulled back.

---

## Purpose & audience

In 30 seconds, convince a hiring manager, recruiter, or engineering leader at a target company that Danny is an exceptional Staff / Lead / Principal iOS candidate. The URL goes in:

- Resume header
- LinkedIn About / Featured
- Cold outreach signature
- "Personal website" fields on applications

This is **not** a Captain SwiftUI marketing site. Not a portfolio of side projects. Not a writing sample collection. The headline product is **Danny the engineer**.

---

## Critical constraint: the Captain SwiftUI tension

Danny owns the Captain SwiftUI brand and has a polished design system already built for it in Claude Design. **Reuse the visual language. Do not reuse the brand identity.**

What this means in practice:

- **Domain and H1 are Danny's name**, not "Captain SwiftUI"
- **No "Captain SwiftUI" wordmark in header or hero**
- Captain SwiftUI appears **once**, in a "Writing & Publications" section, alongside Swift Cookbook — as a credential, not a banner
- **Visual language carries over** — typography, color, motion, any SF Symbols / Apple-platform cues from the existing system. That's Danny's design taste, not branded promotion.
- **No promotion of paid Office Hours, consultation services, or subscriber CTAs.** Mention "2,000+ readers" as credibility, don't ask for signups.
- **One small visual easter egg is acceptable** (e.g., a captain-hat favicon, or a tasteful nautical accent on a single divider). More than one and it reads as a side-business site, which creates compliance optics.

Why this matters: a Schwab employee's personal site that exists to attract employers should read as "professional engineer with a notable side technical writing practice" — not as a Captain SwiftUI promotional surface. The bar is *tasteful*, not *invisible*.

## Tone & voice

- Direct, confident, no swagger
- Numbers, not adjectives
- "Built" / "led" / "shipped" — never "passionate," "results-driven," "thought leader"
- Short paragraphs. Sentences end.
- **One** personality moment allowed (a wry line in the hero, a small wink somewhere). More than one cheapens the rest.

---

## Visual direction

- Inherit the Captain SwiftUI design system: typography, color, motion vocabulary
- Hero should feel **restrained**, not maximalist — this isn't a brand site
- Stat grid is the visual centerpiece — typography-led cards, generous whitespace
- Body type optimized for reading at 14–16px, line length ~60–75ch
- Mobile-first; the audience reads this on iPhone
- Dark mode default if the design system supports it (Apple-developer expectation); light mode toggle nice-to-have
- Liquid Glass material accents are appropriate as long as they read as "Apple-platform-fluent" not "look at my brand"
